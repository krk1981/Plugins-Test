# Carvana Prospect Research Brief

## Executive Summary
Carvana Co (NYSE: CVNA) is a high-growth e-commerce platform revolutionizing used car sales. After a dramatic turnaround from near-bankruptcy, they achieved record 2025 results with $20.3B revenue (+49% YoY) and are now positioned for aggressive expansion toward their goal of 3M annual units by 2030-2035.

---

## Company Overview

### Business Model & Industry Position
- **Industry**: Online Used Car Retail / E-commerce
- **Founded**: 2012, headquartered in Tempe, Arizona
- **Market Position**: Recently added to S&P 500 (December 2025)
- **Core Business**: End-to-end digital platform for buying/selling used cars, disrupting traditional dealerships

### Company Size & Financial Performance
- **Revenue (2025)**: $20.32 billion (+49% YoY)
- **Units Sold (2025)**: 596,641 retail units (+43% YoY)
- **Net Income (2025)**: $1.9 billion (record profitability)
- **Employees**: 7,687 (as of Jan 2026)
- **Q4 2025**: 163,522 units sold, $5.6B revenue

### Key Products & Services
- **Primary**: Online used car marketplace (carvana.com + mobile apps)
- **Ancillary Services**: Vehicle financing, warranty products, vehicle protection plans
- **Unique Assets**: Car vending machines, as-soon-as-next-day delivery
- **Recent Addition**: 56 ADESA auction locations (integrated for inventory/logistics)

## Potential Pain Points & Challenges

### Immediate Operational Challenges (2026)
1. **Reconditioning Capacity Scaling**
   - Higher-than-expected costs in Q4 2025 due to new site openings
   - Need for automation in management processes (3-6 month timeline)
   - Requirement for substantial infrastructure investments

2. **Inventory Management & Supply Chain**
   - Tightening used inventory supply pressuring margins
   - Need to optimize inventory transport and availability
   - Integration challenges with 56 ADESA locations

3. **Technology Scaling Requirements**
   - Massive transaction volume handling capability
   - Real-time inventory management across multiple locations
   - AI-driven customer service optimization
   - Same-day delivery logistics coordination

### Strategic Growth Challenges
4. **Market Pressures**
   - Used car market softening as new vehicle supply recovers
   - Need to offset lower per-unit profitability with higher volumes
   - Competitive pressure from traditional and digital competitors

5. **Execution Risk**
   - Ambitious growth targets (3M units by 2030-2035 at 13.5% EBITDA margin)
   - Balancing rapid expansion with operational efficiency
   - Managing complex logistics and reconditioning operations at scale
